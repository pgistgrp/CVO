There are five steps in the Let's Improve Transportation decision process. In this usability test, you will only participate in Step 1. To learn about how this step fits into the bigger picture, read on.

Step 1: Discuss Concerns

Before we can determine how to best improve the transportation system, we need to know what the problems are. There are two parts of this step:

  • 1a Brainstorm:
    • Participants brainstorm and tag concerns about the transportation system.
  • 1b Review Summaries
    • The moderator identifies themes among these concerns and writes a preliminary summary synthesis of each theme.
    • Participants review and discuss the synthesis, and suggest revisions.
    • The moderator revises themes to reflect participant suggestions, as appropriate.

Step 2: Review Criteria

Criteria are used to evaluate which proposed transportation projects are best suited to address problems with our current transportation system. Researchers identified a list of criteria before this process began, so that data relevant to these criteria could be gathered in advance of the decision process.

  • 2a Review Criteria
    • Particip ants review and discuss criteria in order to evaluate how adequately they reflect each concern theme.
  • 2b Weigh Criteria
    • Participants individually choose their favorite criteria and weigh the relative importance of these criteria for project evaluation.

Step 3: Create Packages
In this step participants review proposed road and transit projects for improving the regional transportation system, and then create their own personal transportation improvement "package". A "transportation package" is simply a set of transportation projects and sources of money to pay for those projects. Decision makers often create packages as options to simplify regional transportation improvement financing decisions.
  • Step 3A: Review Projects
    Prior to this decision process, a panel of transportation specialists have evaluated each of the proposed projects and assigned them with scores for each criterion.
    • Participants review a map of proposed road and transit projects.
    • Participants review information about projects of interest as well as project scores.
    • Participants discuss the appropriateness of project scores and the relative importance of different projects and project alternatives.
  • Step 3b: Select Funding Options
    Here you can read about available funding sources and express their preferences for how funds should be raised to fund a transportation improvment project.
    • Participants review descriptions of funding sources
    • Participants select prefered funding options and see how much money these options would raise. The participant can also see how much money these options would cost him/her per year.
    • Participant can select a maximum amount per year he/she is willing to spend in additional taxes.
  • Step 3c: Create Personal Transportation Package
    Based on the your prefered criteria weights and prefered funding options, a suggested transportation improvment package is displayed. Here you can revise this suggested package to create your own personal transportation package.

Step 4: Evaluate Packages

In order to simplify the decision process, we need to narrow down the number of different packages considered. Therefore, based on participant responses in Step 3, five new packages are automatically constructed. These packages will represent the diversity of participant preferences in terms of both project selection as well as funding source selection. In this step participants evaluate the five new packages and determine which one(s) to collectively endorse.

  •   4a Review Packages
  • Participants can read a brief overview of the processes by which the 5 candidate packages were created.
  • Participants review information about each individual package.
  • Participants discuss the relative benefits and drawbacks of individual packages.
  • 4b Compare Packages
  • Participants use a variety of different tools for comparing the benefits and drawbacks of the five packages, such as cost, spatial equity, and overall scores for each of the criteria.
  • Participants can adjust their criteria weights to see how each package measures up based on their personal weighting choices. They can also compare the ranking of packages based on their own criteria weights to the aggregate criteria weights of all participants.
  •   Participants discuss the potential benefits and drawbacks of the different packages.
  • Participants discuss whether or not they wish to collectively endorse the top-ranked package.
  • The moderator  periodically polls participants to measure  the degree to which a consensus is emerging regarding which package to collectively endorse.
  • At a designated time, all participants are asked to vote to endorse the package currently enjoying the majority of support.
  • Dissenting voters discuss whether or not they wish to include a minority endorsement in the group report. If yes, then they vote on which package to endorse

Step 5: Prepare Group Report

The final step is to compose a report about the results of this decision process which will be delivered to transportation decision makers.
  • The moderator creates a first draft and notifies participants when it is ready.
  • Participants review and discuss this draft report and submit their comments and suggested revisions.
  • The moderator makes revisions and includes participant comments when appropriate.
  • Participants review the final report.
  • All participants vote on whether to approve or disapprove the report. If majority approve the report is forwarded on to decision makers. (Results of this final vote will be included in the report sent on to decision makers.)
